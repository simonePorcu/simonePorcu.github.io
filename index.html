Assistant Professor

University of Cagliari

    Simone Porcu is an Assistant Professor at the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering at the University of Cagliari (Italy). He received a bachelor's degree in computer science from the University of Cagliari, Italy, in 2015, a master's degree in computer science in 2017 and a Ph. D. in Electronic and Computer Engineering from the University of Cagliari in 2021. He also obtained the Doctor Europaeus title for his research period abroad at the Technische Universitat Berlin (September 2018-March 2019). Simone is a member of the Net4U Lab (https://net4u.diee.unica.it/) at the University of Cagliari from 2017, a member of the Italian University Consortium for Telecommunications (CNIT) from 2018, and a member of the IEEE from 2018. He has published five articles in international journals and seven papers on international conferences (see my complete publications list on https://orcid.org/0000-0003-0792-1200). He is an Editor for the IEEE Communication Society Communications-Frontiers, a TPC member of the international conference QoMEX and a TPC member of the 12TH IEEE International Conference On Consumer Technology. He participated as guest editor of the MDPI energies journal special issue. He has been the Vice-Chair of the IEEE Cagliari Student Branch (the year 2019), where six seminars have been organised to improve the engineering students' background and access the whole world of the IEEE. He took part in the organising committee of the 2018 10th International Conference on Quality of Multimedia Experience as the Facility Chair. In 2021 IEEE Internet of Things (IoT) Vertical and Topical Summit on Tourism I served as the Outreach Co-Chair. His research interests are multimedia communication focused on multimedia quality of experience, machine learning, image analysis, and human behaviour.
